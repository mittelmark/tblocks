## Linear Models

- Continous algebraic equations.
- Requires linear relationships or explicit manual transformation.
- Requires converting categories into numbers (dummy variables).
- Risks underfitting complex, interacting data.

## Decision Trees

- Step-functions and Boolean splits.
- Completely immune to non-linear data structures.
- Handles native categorical data cleany and effortlessly.
- Prone to overfitting unless explicitly managed (Pruning or Random Forests).
